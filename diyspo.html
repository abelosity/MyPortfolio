01 brief -

What's the goal here

Design a mobile virtual reality (VR) headset for a specific audience that is primarily built from and utilizes cardboard to take advantage of its unique material properties.

02 opportunity -

Who we're designing for

We decided to focus on the medical field, selecting our user audience to be cancer patients undergoing chemotherapy sessions at a hospital or clinic environment.

03 research -

What's out there

Patients undergoing treatment are often seated in chemotherapy stations. Depending on the facility, there can often be multiple stations situated in a single room. Due to the serious nature of the treatment and fluctuating durations of treatment, patients often come in and out of the room individually, reducing socialization and mingling. Patients often do not have much to do except wait during treatment sessions, and the long durations can become troublesome to many younger patients. We felt that we can develop a VR headset to improve this experience.

04 goals -

What we're trying to do

Lightweight design to prevent fatigue over extended use. Simple setup. Easy to sterilize

05 solution -

Here's our pitch

Diyspo is a lightweight, modular VR headset that is comprised of a solid acrylic shell and a foldable cardboard insert. The headsetâ€™s shell is strong and simple, with face padding and a head strap for comfortable usage. The cardboard insert is laser cut with directions and perforations which can easily be folded to form the exterior shell of the headset. The insert houses the lens and the smartphone device, and locks onto the headset to secure the headset.
