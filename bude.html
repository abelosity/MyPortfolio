01 brief -

What we set out to do.

We recognized a lack in ergonomic design that accommodates arthritis users, affected by a limited range of motion and mobility. The average individual can notice early signs of arthritis as early as at 30 years old. Moreover, as people age, their range of motion and strength in their hands and knees begin to deteriorate.

Our team set out to provide a mindful, simple solution.

02 opportunity -

Approaching tradition, handle up.

Arthritis can gradually increase difficulty for many day to day tasks you perform, such as picking up a spoon or pressing a TV remote. As the symptoms become stronger, you slowly lose control of your precision and strength in movement, making what used to be a simple task now quite slow and masterful. For individuals into the later stages of arthritis, exerting pressure to use objects and have a strong grip can become a demanding task, so we had to start here.

We explored different handle mechanisms to see how simplifiable the traditional flashlight handle could be, taking inspiration from umbrellas hooks to gloves. We wanted to make this easier.

03 process -

How we got here

During this process, I worked on form research and ergonomics of the handle, trying to figure out ways to simplify how one can hold our product. The traditional flashlight handle - a cylinder - is genius. It's simple, easy to produce, and intuitive. However, it does require a steady grip.

What if the product could be molded to hold the hand instead of being held itself?

04 goals -

What we were trying to achieve

Lightweight design to prevent fatigue over extended use.

Versatile handle to accommodate users of varying stages of arthritis symptoms, supporting a wide range of mobility.

Durable to withstand falls, and reduce rolling or unwanted movement.

Multiple orientations of use, adding additional purposes aside from being a single direction handheld flashlight.
